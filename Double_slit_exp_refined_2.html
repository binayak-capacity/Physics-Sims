About the Simulation

The double-slit experiment demonstrates the wave-particle duality of quantum mechanics. When particles pass through two narrow slits, they create an interference pattern on the detection screen—a behavior characteristic of waves.

In Quantum Particle mode, particles exhibit quantum interference, creating alternating bright and dark bands. In Classical Particle mode, they behave classically, forming two distinct bands behind each slit.

Adjust the slit separation and wavelength to see how they affect the interference pattern.
